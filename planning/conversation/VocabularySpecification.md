# "Limelight Yardstick" - Vocabulary Specification

## Overview
This document defines the 16 unique terms used in the Limelight Yardstick rating system. Each term represents a unique combination of a rating category and sentiment level.

---

## Categories

1. **Daring** - How bold, risk-taking, or unconventional are the *story and subject matter*?
2. **Ambition** - How much skill, polish, and effort went into the *production quality*?
3. **Engagement** - How well does the film hold attention and maintain pacing?
4. **Satisfaction** - How much did the film stay with you? Do you want to experience it again? Is time spent on it well-spent?

---

## Sentiment Levels - Detailed Scope

To properly scope the vocabulary, it's important to understand what each sentiment level represents:

### Extremely Negative
**Actively antithetical** — Not just an absence of the quality, but actively seeking to be its opposite, often with malicious or cynical intent.
- **Daring**: Actively propagandist; story exists to push an agenda or defend the status quo
- **Ambition**: Production actively disregards craft; cynically prioritizes profit over care
- **Engagement**: Deliberately dull and draining; designed to waste your time
- **Satisfaction**: Time actively wasted; regrettable decision to watch

### Moderately Negative
**Passively antithetical** — No active effort was made toward the quality, and no care was taken. Not trying to be bad, just not trying at all.
- **Daring**: Conventional and formulaic; no attempt to try anything new
- **Ambition**: Production quality falls short; technically inadequate or poorly executed
- **Engagement**: Sluggish and uneven; pacing issues suggest no effort to maintain momentum
- **Satisfaction**: Unremarkable and forgettable; fails to make any impression

### Moderately Positive
**Genuine effort, imperfect execution** — Clearly trying and making a real attempt, even if not executing perfectly. Shows potential and deserves credit for the genuine effort made.
- **Daring**: Takes risks and explores new ideas, even if not always successful
- **Ambition**: Aims for something significant; clear intent and scope in what it's attempting
- **Engagement**: Engaging and captivating; sustained effort to hold attention
- **Satisfaction**: Memorable and rewarding; the film stays with you

### Extremely Positive
**Near-perfect, successful execution** — Knows the assignment and executes it successfully. Not necessarily transcendent art, but solid execution across everything it attempts. Succeeds in what it's trying to do.
- **Daring**: Story features bold, unconventional subject matter; narrative takes genuine thematic risks
- **Ambition**: Vision successfully realized through technical mastery and flawless execution
- **Engagement**: Riveting and hypnotic; impossible to look away
- **Satisfaction**: Unforgettable and haunting; permanently imprinted on memory

## Vocabulary Matrix

### Daring
How bold, risk-taking, or unconventional are the *story and subject matter*?

#### Extremely Negative
Story/subject actively serves an agenda; defending status quo rather than examining it

- **Propagandist** — Story exists only to serve a predetermined ideological message
- **Dogmatic** — Story presented as doctrine; no room for nuance or questioning
- **Ideological** — Story functions primarily as an ideology delivery mechanism
- **Reactionary** — Story actively defends the status quo against change

#### Moderately Negative
Story/subject is conventional and formulaic; no attempt made to explore new territory

- **Cautious** — Implies deliberate restraint in subject matter
- **Conventional** — Neutral term emphasizing adherence to tradition
- **Formulaic** — Sticks rigidly to proven narrative formulas
- **Predictable** — Follows expected patterns; no surprises in storytelling

#### Moderately Positive
Takes calculated risks; explores new territory

- **Adventurous** — Positive spin on exploration
- **Creative** — Emphasizes originality and innovation
- **Bold** — Strong but not extreme
- **Inventive** — Highlights creative problem-solving

#### Extremely Positive
Fearlessly breaks conventions in story/subject matter; takes genuine narrative and thematic risks

- **Radical** — Story fundamentally reimagines subject matter or narrative conventions
- **Provocative** — Story intentionally provokes and challenges audience assumptions
- **Courageous** — Narrative takes genuine thematic risks; bold subject matter choices
- **Subversive** — Story actively challenges or undermines conventional thinking through its subject
- **Transgressive** — Story crosses boundaries; explores taboo or unconventional themes
- **Challenging** — Story presents difficult ideas or unconventional perspectives

### Ambition
How much skill, polish, and effort went into the *production quality*?

#### Extremely Negative
Production actively disregards craft; cynical approach that prioritizes profit over care

- **Cut-rate** — Hastily made for speed and profit; quality sacrificed for bottom line
- **Shoddy** — Poor quality; deliberately careless execution with disregard for craft
- **Mercenary** — Driven by profit motive; no genuine investment in the work
- **Cheap** — Made cheaply and carelessly; corners cut for financial gain

#### Moderately Negative
Production falls just below acceptable quality; technically inadequate

- **Unrefined** — Rough around the edges; doesn't meet basic production standards
- **Sloppy** — Careless execution; slips and errors visible throughout
- **Rough** — Noticeably unpolished; production values are subpar
- **Amateurish** — Lacks professional polish; shows inexperience or lack of resources
- **Shabby** — Shows wear and lack of care; production quality deteriorates

#### Moderately Positive
Production shows clear ambition; aimed for something significant and meaningful

- **Ambitious** — Clear intent to accomplish something substantial
- **Purposeful** — Shows intentional scope and vision in what it's attempting
- **Aspiring** — Reaches for meaningful goals even if imperfectly executed
- **Expansive** — Attempts to do something beyond routine; aims for broader vision

#### Extremely Positive
Production successfully executes the ambitious vision; delivers on technical excellence

- **Masterful** — Expert-level technical execution realizes the ambitious vision
- **Immaculate** — Flawless attention to craft and production detail throughout
- **Virtuosic** — Vision realized through extraordinary technical skill and precision
- **Flawless** — Production execution is technically perfect; achieves intended scope
- **Precision-Crafted** — Ambitious scope realized through meticulous technical execution
- **Executed** — Vision fully and cleanly brought to the screen through skilled craftsmanship

### Engagement
How well does the film hold attention and maintain pacing?

#### Extremely Negative
Film actively drains engagement; designed to waste your time

- **Tedious** — Deliberately dull; drains attention through repetition
- **Punishing** — Pacing is hostile to the viewer's time and attention
- **Exhausting** — Film actively depletes your mental energy
- **Interminable** — Feels designed to be as long and draining as possible

#### Moderately Negative
Film fails to maintain engagement; pacing suffers from inattention

- **Sluggish** — Implies slow, uneven pacing; no effort to maintain momentum
- **Meandering** — Lacks focus; pacing suggests no care for viewer attention
- **Uneven** — Inconsistent pacing; attention not prioritized
- **Languid** — Slow, dragging quality suggesting lack of purpose

#### Moderately Positive
Compelling; holds attention throughout

- **Captivating** — Emphasizes drawing and holding interest
- **Engaging** — Direct and clear descriptor
- **Compelling** — Emphasizes power to hold attention
- **Absorbing** — Suggests immersive quality

#### Extremely Positive
Gripping; absolutely impossible to look away

- **Riveting** — Intense, captivating descriptor
- **Electrifying** — Suggests energetic, thrilling quality
- **Hypnotic** — Emphasizes inability to look away
- **Magnetic** — Emphasizes irresistible pull of attention

### Satisfaction
How much did the film stay with you? Do you want to experience it again? Is time spent on it well-spent?

#### Extremely Negative
Time actively wasted; you wish you'd never watched it

- **Regrettable** — Active regret; time was genuinely wasted
- **Noxious** — Left you worse off for having watched it
- **Draining** — Actively took something from you rather than added
- **Contemptible** — Deserves contempt for wasting your time

#### Moderately Negative
Film fails to make an impression; you forget it immediately

- **Unremarkable** — Nothing stands out or lingers; passive forgetting
- **Disposable** — Forgotten as soon as credits roll
- **Unmemorable** — Fails to create any lasting impression
- **Ephemeral** — Fleeting; actively forgotten, not recalled

#### Moderately Positive
Stays with you; you'd gladly watch it again

- **Memorable** — Sticks with you; creates lasting impression
- **Rewarding** — Time investment pays off emotionally
- **Lingering** — Stays in your thoughts after viewing
- **Resonant** — Creates emotional echo that persists

#### Extremely Positive
Unforgettable; you can't stop thinking about it; immediate urge to rewatch

- **Unforgettable** — Permanently imprinted on memory
- **Haunting** — Stays with you; can't get it out of your mind
- **Transcendent** — Transforms how you think; stays with you
- **Indelible** — Leaves permanent emotional mark

## Selecting Final Vocabulary

For each of the 16 category-level combinations, **3-4 term options** are provided above. The selection process should consider:

1. **Level alignment** — Ensure Extremely Negative terms are actively antithetical, Moderately Negative terms are passively absent, Moderately Positive terms show genuine effort, and Extremely Positive terms demonstrate successful execution
2. **Tone consistency** — Selected terms should feel like they belong in the same "voice"
3. **Uniqueness** — Each of the 16 final terms must be distinct and non-overlapping
4. **Combination readability** — How well do terms work together in example ratings?
4. **Cultural appropriateness** — Do terms work across diverse film cultures?

Once final terms are selected, create a final vocabulary list from one option per level per category.

## Notes on Vocabulary Choices

### Level Scope Framework
The vocabulary is organized around a framework of four distinct levels:
- **Extremely Negative** = Actively antithetical (malicious intent, cynicism, or active opposition)
- **Moderately Negative** = Passively absent (no effort, no care, but not actively bad)
- **Moderately Positive** = Genuine effort (clearly trying, showing potential, deserves credit)
- **Extremely Positive** = Successful execution (knows the assignment, delivers on it)

This distinction is critical: Moderately Negative is not "slightly bad," it's "didn't try." Moderately Positive is not "mediocre," it's "genuinely making an effort." This framework applies across all categories.

### Approach
- **Multiple suggestions per level** provide options that vary in tone, emphasis, and specificity
- Each suggestion includes a brief note explaining its particular angle or emphasis
- This allows flexibility in selecting the final vocabulary that best matches the desired voice
- All suggestions are vetted against the level scope to ensure they represent the correct distinction

### Tone & Voice
- All terms are **evaluative** (express a judgment) but operate on a **spectrum of intent**
- Active vs. passive distinction is built into the framework, not just the terms
- Terms avoid redundancy with their definitions
- Within negative terms, emphasis is placed on intent (active malice vs. passive neglect)
- Within positive terms, emphasis is placed on effort (trying vs. succeeding)

### Balance
- **Negative terms** range from passively uninspired to actively antagonistic
- **Positive terms** range from earnest effort to masterful execution
- Progression is consistent: Extremely Negative → Moderately Negative → Moderately Positive → Extremely Positive

### Consistency
- Terms within each category maintain parallel structure (predominantly single-word adjectives)
- Sentiment progression is consistent across all categories
- Level scope is applied uniformly across Daring, Ambition, Engagement, and Satisfaction

### Extensibility
- Future customization could allow users to create domain-specific vocabularies
- Current set is suitable for general film analysis
- Level scope provides a framework for generating new vocabulary sets
