# Limelight yardstick levels

Let's add some context to each of the levels to better scope. The terms currently the the extremely positive and moderately positive terms. Mostly field too. Hyperbolically positive and the extremely negative terms. Feel like they belong in the moderately negative and the and are not hyperbolic enough.

Let's start by looking at the negative terms. Moderately negative should mean passively antithetical. In other words, the opposite of daring but not actively so. Same thing with ambitious: it's not lazy but it's not trying to be ambitious; no attempt was made, no care was taken but it's not actively trying to be the opposite. Extremely negative should be actively antithetical: not just *not* daring but actively propagandaist, actively in defense of the status quo as opposed to picking apart. Same thing with ambitious: it's not just *not* making an attempt; it's actively seeking do things badly, not for entertainment but for making a quick buck.

Moderately positive should be exactly that. It should be moderate; not mediocre but showing potential. It's not executing at all cylinders; it's making it attempt even if it's not succeeding. It's still putting in genuine effort and it should get some credit for that.

Extremely positive. Should be near perfect in execution. It should be successful at what it is seeking to do. It doesn't necessarily mean it's exactly art; it means it knows the assignment and it is successful in everything that it's attempting to do. Moderately positive is for making a genuine effort; extremely positive is for solid execution.
